Resource

This section lists some classic and useful resources of MRI, mainly focusing on pulse sequences and image reconstruction. These resources help me a lot during my learning journey as an MRI person. Think hard, go deep, and embrace the details

  • Physical Basis & Pulse sequences
    • Bernstein MA et al. Handbook of MRI Pulse Sequences, 2004 Elsevier. (Book)
    • Haacke EM et al. Magnetic Resonance Imaging: Physical Principles and Sequence Design, 2014 Wiley-Blackwell. (Book)
    • Dwight Nishimura. Priciples of Magnetic Resonance Imaging, 2010. (Book)
    • Daniel Ennis & Brian Hargreaves. Stanford RAD229 (Course Website)
  • Medical image reconstruction & Inverse Problem
    • John M. Pauly's Medical Images Reconstruction EE369C. Classical!
    • Gilbert Strang's Linear Algebra MIT18.065, helpful for MR image reconstruction and Machine Learning. It's enjoyable listening to Prof. Strang's lectures.
    • Inverse Problems (appliable to rapid MR recon):[Purdue:ECE641][CMU: Youtube|Website]
  • Reconstruction Tools
    • Trajectory:
      • Brian Hargreaves: Variable-Density Spiral Design Functions (in Matlab & C)
      • Miki Lustig: Time Optimal Gradient Design (in Matlab)
      • Nicolas Chauffert: Gradient Waveform Design (in Matlab)
    • Simulation:
      • Extended phase graphs (EPG):
    • MR Image Reconstruction Tools:
      • Sigpy: a python-based package for iterative image reconstruction.
      • BART: a free and open-source image-reconstruction framework for Computational Magnetic Resonance Imaging.
